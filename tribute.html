Frank "The Voice" Sinatra

Influential Jazz Singer


Timeline of Frank Sinatra's life:

  • 1915 - Born in Hoboken, New Jersey, U.S.
  • 1934 - Met his first wife, Nancy Rose Barbarto at the age of 19.
  • Summer 1935 - Sinatra took Nancy to see his idol Bing Crosby. Sinatra says "After seeing him that night, I knew I had to be a singer."
  • 1935 - First big break when Sinatra and local trio toured together as the Hoboken Four.
  • 1939 - Married Nancy Barbato in Jersey City.
  • 1940 - Debuting at New York's Paramount Theatre. Sinatra sang to a large crowd wit the Dorsey band.
  • 1954 - Wins the Oscar for Best Supporting Actor from "From Here To Eternity"
  • 1959 - Won 3 Grammy Awards for "Come Dance With Me!"
  • 1966 - "Strangers in the Night" single hits #1. "That's Life" single hits #4.
  • 1973 - The Songwriters of America named Frank Sinatra Entertainer of the Century.
  • 1985 - Received the Presidential Medal of Freedom awarded by Ronald Reagan.
  • 1990 - Received the Ella, a lifetime achievement award bestowed upon him by the Society of Singers.
  • 1998 - Passes away at age 82.

"He was the epitome of what singing is all about, beautiful sounds, smooth as silk, effortless, impeccable phrasing, stylish, intelligent and full of heart."

Barbara Streisand
